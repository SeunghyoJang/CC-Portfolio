Project#3 Experimental Clock

Description

What I wanted to express through an experimental clock is the psychological pressure on me over time. I feel a lot of pressure about homework. It is often delayed due to the burden of doing well and the psychological pressure of the pressure. However, as time goes by, the burden on me increases. Seconds accumulate to become minutes, and minutes accumulate to become an hour. As time goes by, the color of my face turns redder is an expression of my psychological burden.

Reflection & Process

What I feel every week while coding as a beginner is that it is difficult to execute as it was planned and sketched. The result always appears different from what I sketched before and there is always a variable errors in the process. My first plan was to divide the canvas into three parts, seconds, minutes, and time. The faces were gradually piling up in each section. After 60 seconds pass, 1 face comes down from minute section, and when 60 minutes were piled up, 1 face from hour section has to came down. Different proportions of face sizes were applied to each, causing the face to come down in the order of the hour, minutes and seconds. However, the size of the circle suitable for each section could not be applied, so first, all the same face sizes were applied to all seconds, minutes, and time. (With the addition of milliseconds, the sections are divided into quarters.) Instead of stacking 60 seconds and then 1 minute coming down, each section was filled with 100 circles with x:5, y:20. The method of filling the color is now.get Milliseconds(), now.get Seconds(), now.getMinutes(), and now.get Hours(), which are applied by coloring the face based on the current time. The change in face color as the unit of time increases was applied to each section. I want to figure it a little more about the how to apply different facial expressions and the change in the size of the circle for each time unit.
